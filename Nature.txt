Nature is the beautiful and essential world around us that includes everything not made by humans—trees, rivers, mountains, animals, air, soil, and sunlight. It is the foundation of life on Earth, providing us with food, water, oxygen, and shelter.
Forests are often called the “lungs of the Earth” because they produce oxygen and absorb carbon dioxide. Rivers and oceans support countless aquatic creatures and also help maintain the Earth’s climate. Mountains give rise to rivers and are home to many unique plants and animals.
Nature is also deeply connected to human well-being. Spending time in natural environments can reduce stress, improve mood, and increase creativity. The sounds of birds, the sight of green trees, and the freshness of clean air all give peace to the mind.However, nature is under threat due to pollution, deforestation, and climate change. It is important for us to protect and preserve it by planting trees, reducing waste, saving water, and avoiding plastic use.
In short, nature is not just our surroundings—it is our life source. Protecting nature means protecting our future. 🌿
